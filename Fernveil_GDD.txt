1. Introduction
One-liner
A cozy greenhouse sim by day and a fantasy crafting shop by night — grow strange plants, craft
remedies for eerie customers, and help a town slowly remember what it tried to forget.

Player experience goals
•

Cozy routine that feels comforting and productive.

•

A steady, creeping mystery that escalates with progress.

•

Choices that matter, without overwhelming branching complexity.

Design pillars
1. Greenhouse-first management (curated planting, upgrades, atmosphere).
2. Night shift narrative (customers drive story progression).
3. Plants with traits (effects matter mechanically and narratively).
4. Light–medium consequences (meters plus a few flags, not endless branches).
5. Tiny outdoor support plot (utility crops fuel crafting; prevents scope creep).

Target platform
•

PC (Steam / itch.io).

•

Optional controller support.

2. Legal
Ownership & IP

Third-party content

Teen: unsettling themes, paranormal imagery, mild dread. No extreme gore required.

Setting
A quiet town at the forest’s edge. Your greenhouse sits on old glass foundations — locals say

Visual style
•

Day: warm lighting, soft edges, inviting clutter, gentle UI.

•

Night: cool tones, fog, candle/glow lighting, reflections emphasized.

Art Direction: Color Palette

Art Direction: Pixel Art Style Reference
• 2D pixel art with painterly lighting and high-contrast glow accents (cozy night atmosphere).
• Top-down perspective with readable silhouettes and dense environmental detail (plants, props,
textures).
• Lighting emphasis: warm lantern pools against cool ambient moonlight; saturated glow used
sparingly for magical elements.
• Color usage: muted base tones with selective neon/bioluminescent highlights (mushrooms,
fireflies, veil effects).

High concept
Run a greenhouse shop. By crafting items from plants with supernatural traits, you fulfill night
requests that restore the town memories.

Competitive differentiators

•

Day/night character shift (same NPC, different persona as Memory grows).

•

Meaningful outcomes without massive branching (meters and key flags).

Technology

4. Gameplay
Core loop
Day → Prepare → Night → Resolve
6. Day: tend greenhouse, harvest, maintain outdoor support plot, visit town, forage forest edge.
7. Prepare: craft inventory, stock shelves, plan night-only planting, choose stability vs profit.
8. Night: shop opens; strange customers request effects; you choose how to fulfill.
9. Resolve: earn money, update meters, unlock story/recipes/areas, trigger veil events.

Simulation systems
Progression meters
•

Memory (0–100): Town recalls truth; unlocks areas, dialogue, lore, and recipes.

•

Veil Pressure (0–100): Paranormal intensity; increases rare customers plus risk events.

•

Trust (per key NPC, 0–5 hearts): Affects help, discounts, special quests, and ending variants.

Scope guardrail rule
To prevent scope creep, the game uses a clear split:
•

Greenhouse = supernatural/story plants.

•

Outdoor support plot = utility crops only (binders, stabilizers, compost inputs, ward
ingredients).

Economy
•

Cozy baseline: predictable earnings from standard goods.

•

High-margin: rare night customers and risky recipes.

•

Outdoor utility crops reduce bottlenecks and enable planning choices.

Player Interactions
Animal Husbandry (Micro-Barn System)
• Fernveil includes a small-scale animal system to support a farm vibe without expanding into a
full ranch simulator.
• Animals primarily provide crafting ingredients (milk, eggs, honey) used in teas, tonics, and
salves. The system is greenhouse-adjacent and intentionally limited in scope.
Core Loop
• Feed → Care → Collect → Craft.
• Daily interactions are quick (1–2 actions per animal) to preserve the cozy pacing.
Buildings and Capacity
• Micro-Barn (courtyard-attached): starts with 1 stall and upgrades up to 4 stalls total.
• Coop corner (optional, can share barn footprint): 2–6 chickens depending on tier.
• Beehive box (outdoor courtyard): 1–3 hives depending on tier; passive collection. Animal Types
and Outputs (Starter Set) Cow
• Primary output: Milk (daily).
• Rare output: Cream (chance increased by care).

• Veil interaction (optional): “Veil-Kissed Milk” may appear when Veil Pressure is high or after
certain moon events—powerful but risky ingredient. Chicken
• Primary output: Eggs (frequent).
• Use: binding agents for recipes, baked comfort items, or stabilizers for risky concoctions.
Beehive
• Primary output: Honey (passive, collected every few days).
• Use: sweetener base for teas/tonics; improves “comfort” outcomes and some customer
satisfaction results. Crafting Integration (Examples)
• Witchmint Milk Tonic: calm/clarity; supports Trust-positive solutions.
• Moonflower Latte (night-only): concealment; increases Veil slightly.
• Ashbloom Cream Salve: cleansing/removes taint; high-value late-game craft.
• Honeyed Dusk Tea: sleep/dream control; safer alternative to risky nightshade paths.
Replayability Hooks
• Daily Orders Board can include animal-based requests (e.g., “2 Milk + 1 Calming Tea”).
• Weekly Market Day can feature category boosts (e.g., “Dairy Week” or “Honey Week”).
• Greenhouse Challenges may require animal ingredients (e.g., “Craft 3 tonics using milk
base”).
• Full Moon Favors can occasionally request a specific offering (e.g., warm milk infused with
Silver Sage). Scope Guardrails
• No breeding/genetics systems.
• No complex health/disease simulation; care is simple and positive (pet/brush/feed).
• Animals remain courtyard/barn-contained; no wide roaming AI.
• Animals serve crafting and atmosphere first; the greenhouse remains the primary gameplay
center.

6. Story
Premise
The greenhouse is more than a business — it is a boundary. The town once made a pact to keep
something in the forest contained. The ledger holds “care instructions” that read like ritual
notes. As you succeed, the town memories return… and so does the thing those memories were
built to hide.

Act structure

Soft Acts (Internal Chapters)
• Acts are used for pacing and production planning, but the UI does not show “Act 1/2/3.”
Players experience progression as the town and nights change over time.
• Act progression uses clear milestone objectives (not hidden grind thresholds). Milestone-based
progression
• Early → Midgame (Act 1 → Act 2) example trigger: Complete 1 Full Moon Favor, resolve 3
recurring customer outcomes (not random orders), and discover 5 Memory Fragments.
• Midgame → Late game (Act 2 → Act 3) example trigger: Unlock the Old Conservatory,
complete 2 Full Moon Favors total, and resolve 2 key NPC night revelations. What changes
between chapters
• Customer pool expands (new request types + 1–2 new recurring customers).
• New plant families and recipes unlock.
• Town ambience updates (dialogue lines, small prop swaps, subtle environmental tells).
• Veil events intensify primarily through Veil Pressure so escalation still reflects player choices.
Cozy freedom preserved
• Players can linger in the cozy loop: key story progression waits until a clearly marked story
request/favor is accepted.
• Optional pacing tools: “end day early” and reduced story-customer frequency if the player
declines story hooks.
Act 1 — The Glass Breathes
•

Establish day/night loop and basic crafting.

•

Introduce first recurring customers (3).

•

First full moon: Garden Favor appears.

•

Reveal: condensation forms symbols on the glass that match the ledger.

Act 1 closing beat: a townsfolk recognizes you at night and insists you have been here before.
Act 2 — Names Return
•

Memory fragments appear in NPC dialogue and locations.

•

Night versions of characters become more direct.

•

Unlock: Old Conservatory Ruins.

•

Ledger pages collected as Memory Fragments.

Act 2 closing beat: you discover the last keeper vanished — and the greenhouse was the lock.
Act 3 — The Veil Thins
•

Rare plants introduce higher-risk crafting.

•

Veil events intensify if pressure is high.

•

Key choice: stabilize the pact, bargain with it, or break it.

Act 3 closing beat: the garden demands a final favor.

Endings (light–medium)
Three major endings, each with small variations based on Trust and a few key flags:
•

Seal the Veil (stability ending): Memory high, Veil managed.

•

Bargain (compromise ending): medium Memory, strong Trust with key NPC(s).

•

Break the Pact (risk ending): Veil high, dangerous solutions favored.

Variations reflect who stands with you, what you sacrificed, and whether the town truly
remembers.

Replayability Systems
• Fernveil supports two valid playstyles: (1) relaxed farming/crafting “grind” loops and (2)
quest driven progression that unlocks new content faster.
• Repeatable systems provide structure and variety without requiring heavy branching. Daily
Orders Board (Day)
• 3–5 standard orders per day (common goods, utility crops, basic remedies).
• Primary rewards: money + common materials; occasional seed packets.

• Designed for chill play sessions and steady upgrade progress.
Night Requests (Rotating Pool)
• Night customers are a mix of random repeatable and recurring story customers.
• Weekly rotation changes which request types appear (helps runs feel different).
• Multiple solution options (safe vs risky) meaningfully affect Veil/Trust outcomes. Weekly
Market Day (Day Event)
• Once per week, one product category gets boosted demand/prices (e.g., Tea Week, Bouquet
Week, Ward Week).
• Rewards: high profit opportunities + occasional rare utility seeds. • Encourages planning and
variety in planting/crafting.
Full Moon Favors (Milestone Event)
• The garden requests a major favor; completing it grants significant unlocks or permanent
perks.
• Optional timing: favors can be delayed, but ignoring them raises Veil Pressure and increases
haunting events.
• Keeps the story spine present without forcing fast progression. Greenhouse Challenges
(Optional Objectives)
• Rotating weekly challenges (grow under conditions, fulfill themed orders, harvest targets).
• Rewards: cosmetics, rare cuttings, or upgrade components.
• Turns grinding into satisfying mini-goals. Collection Journal (Long-Term Completion)
• Track discovered plants, recipes, customer types, and Memory Fragments.
• Milestone rewards at 25/50/75/100% completion (cosmetics, rare seeds, special visitors).
• Supports post-story play and completion-focused replayability.
Scope Guardrails

• Outdoor support plot remains intentionally small and utility-focused to preserve the
greenhouse first identity and prevent farm-sim scope creep.
• Repeatable systems always pay out at least one meaningful reward (money, useful
materials/seeds, cosmetics, or permanent upgrades) to avoid “busywork.”
